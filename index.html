Future Electronics

Chapter 1

Where are we?

While tech stocks have dominated the market in recent times and the hottest new tech - AI is foremost in everyones minds, (consumer) technology has not felt more discordant or perhaps even dystopian. The very platforms that were meant to build connections and enable borderless communication to a degree that humanity could have scarcely imagined before, are addictive, caustic and divisive. The front page of the internet is an unusable mess and the company behind it is being sued for monopolistic practices. And this is just for starters. Even if you set aside the head line grabbing stuff, the day to day reality is just so unhealthy - attention is at an all time low. Though I have no social media, I often catch myself stuck in a doom scroll of YouTube Shorts. One can only imagine how much worse it is for the average person with Instagram, FaceBook, TikTok, Snapchat etc.

I remember back in the '00s when the internet was first becoming widely available, it was such an incredibly exciting thing to go online. BB forums, IRC chat rooms, RSS feeds, Yahoo Messenger, Google circles even. It was a magical time. The internet felt more open, less controlled/manufactured and just more genuine. The early internet was ironically however considered to be generally untrustworthy especially when it came to information. Yet, we now find ourselves now drowning in more information, misinformation and disinformation. This might be what finally breaks the internet for most people - with AI, generating content indistinguishable from reality means that we can fully expect the dead internet theory to become real. This reality has also mutilated the hardware and devices we use.

Where do we go from here?

This is not a thing that affects some of us in some superficial way - it is affecting all of us in an extreme but perhaps not so obvious way. Electronics are an inextricably a foundational part of modern society and is as important a thing as any that exists. But i'm not here to sell you on a tale of gloom! "Problems are just an opportunity in disguise" as they say and in this case, not just an opportunity but an incredible call to action. Creating change is an incredibly incredibly hard thing to do. Thus the arrival of a disruptive technology where new capabilities are unlocked and existing paradigms are challenged are golden opportunities - and not to be missed! We are luckily in the midst of 3 revolutionary technologies (AI, XR and decentralized protocols‚Å∫). I believe the confluence of these will enable a far better paradigm.

Sitting in room, hunched over a laptop, sifting through google results to get to ad infested pages to glean whats the best vacuum cleaner to buy... can now instead become a stroll through the park, walking the dog while conversing with an AI assistant.

Journaling, again hunched over a desk or a computer (and doing so infrequently as its a time intensive, unergonomic and annoying task) can instead be a hike in the woods dictating notes without being constrained by the medium and maintaining emotional content, ability to edit and update.

Jumping from app to app to order a meal from your favorite restaurant and plugging in your credit card information to yet another site, can now instead be as simple as a text or voice command.

But these things are not a given, these new technologies like all before them are not somehow inherently good. In fact, they very well could be used to further perpetuate the bad paradigms that exist. And that's where we come in - realizing this new vision requires people to believe in it, build it and fight for it. And the reality is that its not some grand challenge or final boss to beat but just mindful, intentional product design and the many, seemingly minor decisions that go into it. Do it right and we can have it all - ergonomics, a way out of sinking in information, work-life balance, the existence nay the thriving of democratic society, you name it.

And this is where I want to go.

Roshan M
